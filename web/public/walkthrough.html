Reflective Lens

A walkthrough for coaches and players

Two separate journeys in one app. Coaches reflect on their sessions. Players reflect on their own games, privately, and the two never mix. Both work the same way. You capture your thoughts in your own words, by speaking or typing, answer a gentle prompt or two for a little more, and see it reflected back. Notes, reflections and answers can all be a voice note or text. Nothing is ever graded.

A mirror, not a verdict

Coach From a session to a reflection

Set your intent and pick your squad, capture notes as they happen by voice or text, record the result, then reflect. The app only asks for a little more where a note is thin, and always in your own language.

Output What the reports look like

This is the payoff. Every report restates only what you and your notes actually said. It reviews what you hoped to see, honestly, and reflects your own words back. Nothing is invented, nothing is graded. The examples below are illustrative.

Coach, match report

U12 Lions vs Ashwood

You wanted brave build-up and calmer heads under pressure. The build-up looked good. The pressure still rushes them.
Result: won 3-1, at home. Saturday 12 July.

What you hoped to see

  • ✓ Keeper starts the moves. Noted twice, and calmly.
  • ~ Centre-backs split wide. There in the first half, less so in the second.
  • ✗ Calm under pressure. Rushed it once and lost the ball in midfield.

What stood out

  • Two of the three goals started with the keeper playing short.
  • The panic came when Ashwood pressed with two, not one.

Noted for next

  • Rehearse the press-release you flagged.
  • Give the centre-backs a retreat option.
Drawn from your notes and your reflection. Export as a PDF to share.
